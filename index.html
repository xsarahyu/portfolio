About Me

  • As a biologist turned software engineer, I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University, where I developed DNA software to analyze the p53 tumor suppressor gene. Simultaneously, I founded Carnegie Mellon Science Olympiad, a national STEM competition promoting equitable access to education. My experiences endow me with an analytical mind and a meticulous eye for detail, all of which are reflected in my user-centric approach to software engineering.
  • My recent project analyzes users' DNA for genetic predisposition to Alzheimer's disease, transforming scientific jargon into straightforward yet comprehensive conclusions. Beyond an app, it signifies my commitment to a world where knowledge is not only discovered, but also shared and understood by all.
  • Let's connect and explore how we can leverage technology to create accessible and empowering tools.
